## Overview

This article presents a hands-on blueprint for learning about recent advances in natural language processing through practical projects rather than theoretical study first. The covered topics include transformers, language models like BERT and GPT-2, evaluation benchmarks, and conversational interfaces.

## Getting Started

The author outlines an initial project list:

- Experiments with OpenAI's GPT-2 to understand language models
- Building an ML-powered chatbot to explore natural language understanding
- Creating a tool to transcribe and analyze audio conversations

The approach was deliberately open-ended, allowing for refinement as interests evolved.

## Playing Around with GPT-2

### Parody Twitter Bot

The author created a GPT-2 chatbot trained on tweets from multiple parody accounts, including personas of Queen Elizabeth, Lord Voldemort, Darth Vader, and Bored Elon Musk. The resulting bot account combined these personas into "@ai_gpt2."

### Evaluating NLP Benchmarks

The author explored standard benchmarks like GLUE and SQuAD to understand how language models are evaluated. These benchmarks test different language aspects including entity recognition, question-answering, and coreference resolution.

## Practical NLU: Conversational Interfaces

### AI Voice Assistant

Following RASA's tutorial, the author built a voice-enabled assistant using open-source tools, exploring how developers approach conversational AI.

### Customer Service Chatbot

The author then experimented with Google's Dialogflow managed service, discovering it straightforward after understanding core concepts like intents and actions.

## Concluding Thoughts

The author emphasizes the importance of exploring breadth over depth initially, allowing for efficient learning within time constraints. Key observations include:

- Voice interfaces are gaining adoption, especially in emerging economies
- Natural language improvements will aid technology democratization
- We should remain realistic about current limitations despite recent hype
- RASA suggests "we are perhaps a decade away" from the highest level of conversational AI maturity

The author advocates for responsible innovation in language technology while continuing to document learnings publicly.
